# Common Crop Diseases — Identification and Treatment

## Rice Diseases

### Rice Blast (Leaf Blast)
- **Symptoms**: Diamond-shaped spots on leaves with gray centers and dark borders.
- **Cause**: Fungus *Magnaporthe oryzae*.
- **Favorable conditions**: High humidity, excess nitrogen, cool temperatures.
- **Treatment**: Apply Tricyclazole 75 WP @ 0.6 g/L. Use resistant varieties. Avoid excess nitrogen.
- **Prevention**: Balanced fertilization, seed treatment with fungicide, resistant varieties (KRH-4).

### Brown Spot
- **Symptoms**: Brown oval spots with gray centers on leaves.
- **Cause**: Fungus *Bipolaris oryzae*.
- **Treatment**: Spray Mancozeb 75 WP @ 2.5 g/L. Apply potassium fertilizer.
- **Prevention**: Balanced NPK, avoid water stress, use clean seed.

### Sheath Blight
- **Symptoms**: Large oval lesions on leaf sheaths near water level.
- **Cause**: Fungus *Rhizoctonia solani*.
- **Treatment**: Spray Hexaconazole 5 EC @ 2 mL/L.
- **Prevention**: Avoid dense planting, drain excess water.

## Tomato Diseases

### Early Blight
- **Symptoms**: Dark brown concentric ring spots on lower leaves.
- **Cause**: Fungus *Alternaria solani*.
- **Treatment**: Spray Chlorothalonil or Mancozeb @ 2.5 g/L.
- **Prevention**: Mulching, crop rotation, remove infected debris.

### Late Blight
- **Symptoms**: Water-soaked lesions on leaves, white mold on underside.
- **Cause**: Oomycete *Phytophthora infestans*.
- **Treatment**: Spray Metalaxyl + Mancozeb @ 2.5 g/L.
- **Prevention**: Avoid overhead irrigation, use resistant varieties.

### Bacterial Wilt
- **Symptoms**: Sudden wilting of entire plant, browning of vascular tissue.
- **Cause**: Bacterium *Ralstonia solanacearum*.
- **Treatment**: No chemical cure. Remove infected plants. Soil solarization.
- **Prevention**: Use resistant varieties, crop rotation with non-solanaceous crops.

## Potato Diseases

### Late Blight
- **Symptoms**: Water-soaked spots, white mold, rapid plant death.
- **Treatment**: Spray Mancozeb @ 2.5 g/L every 7 days.
- **Prevention**: Use certified seed, hill up tubers, destroy volunteer plants.

### Early Blight
- **Symptoms**: Brown spots with concentric rings on older leaves.
- **Treatment**: Spray Chlorothalonil @ 2 g/L.
- **Prevention**: Crop rotation, balanced fertilization.

## General Disease Management Principles

1. **Use certified disease-free seeds and planting material.**
2. **Practice crop rotation** — avoid planting the same family crops consecutively.
3. **Maintain field sanitation** — remove crop residues and weed hosts.
4. **Balance nutrition** — excess nitrogen promotes fungal diseases.
5. **Optimize irrigation** — avoid waterlogging and overhead watering.
6. **Monitor regularly** — scout fields weekly for early symptoms.
7. **When using chemicals**: Read labels, wear protective gear, observe waiting periods.

## Safety Precautions for Pesticide Use
- Always wear gloves, mask, and long-sleeved clothing.
- Do not eat, drink, or smoke while spraying.
- Spray in the direction of wind, preferably in morning or evening.
- Wash hands and face thoroughly after application.
- Store pesticides away from food and children.
- Observe the pre-harvest interval (PHI) strictly.
