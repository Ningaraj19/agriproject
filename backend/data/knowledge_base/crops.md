# Agriculture Knowledge Base — Crops and Farming Practices

## Major Crops in Karnataka

### Rice (Paddy)
Rice is the staple food crop of Karnataka, primarily grown in the coastal and Malnad regions. 
- **Season**: Kharif (June–November) is the main season; Rabi crop is grown in irrigated areas.
- **Soil**: Well-drained, fertile clay or clay loam soils with pH 5.5–6.5.
- **Water**: Requires 1200–1500 mm of rainfall or irrigation.
- **Varieties**: Jaya, IR-64, KRH-4 (hybrid), Sona Masuri.
- **Spacing**: 20 cm × 15 cm for transplanted rice.
- **Fertilizer**: NPK ratio 120:60:60 kg/ha for high-yield varieties.
- **Harvest**: 120–150 days after sowing.

### Ragi (Finger Millet)
Ragi is a nutritious millet crop extensively grown in Karnataka.
- **Season**: Kharif (July–November).
- **Soil**: Red sandy loam, laterite soils; tolerates poor soils.
- **Water**: 350–500 mm rainfall; drought-tolerant.
- **Varieties**: GPU-28, GPU-48, ML-365, Indaf-15.
- **Spacing**: 30 cm × 10 cm.
- **Fertilizer**: NPK 50:40:25 kg/ha.
- **Benefits**: Rich in calcium, iron, and dietary fiber.

### Sugarcane
Major cash crop in northern Karnataka districts.
- **Season**: Planted January–March; harvested after 12–14 months.
- **Soil**: Deep, well-drained loamy soils.
- **Water**: 1500–2500 mm; needs regular irrigation.
- **Varieties**: Co-86032, CoC-671, Co-62175.
- **Fertilizer**: NPK 250:100:120 kg/ha.
- **Intercropping**: Can be intercropped with pulses and vegetables.

### Tomato
High-value vegetable crop grown across Karnataka.
- **Season**: Can be grown year-round with irrigation.
- **Soil**: Well-drained sandy loam with pH 6.0–7.0.
- **Varieties**: Arka Rakshak, Arka Samrat, PKM-1.
- **Spacing**: 60 cm × 45 cm.
- **Fertilizer**: NPK 120:80:80 kg/ha with organic manure.

## Soil Health Management

### Soil Testing
- Test soil every 2–3 years from each field.
- Collect samples from 15 cm depth at 10–15 spots.
- Test for pH, organic carbon, N, P, K, and micronutrients.
- Use Soil Health Card recommendations for fertilizer application.

### Organic Matter
- Apply Farm Yard Manure (FYM): 10–15 tonnes/ha.
- Green manuring with sunhemp or dhaincha.
- Vermicomposting for nutrient-rich organic fertilizer.

### Crop Rotation
- Rotate cereals with legumes to fix nitrogen.
- Example: Rice → Pulses → Vegetables → Rice.
- Helps break pest and disease cycles.

## Irrigation Methods

### Drip Irrigation
- Most efficient method; 90% water use efficiency.
- Ideal for vegetables, fruits, and cash crops.
- Government subsidy available: up to 55% for small farmers.

### Sprinkler Irrigation
- Suitable for field crops like wheat, pulses.
- 70–80% water use efficiency.
- Good for undulating terrain.

### Furrow Irrigation
- Traditional method for row crops.
- 40–50% efficiency; higher water usage.
- Best for sugarcane and maize.

## Integrated Pest Management (IPM)

### Principles
1. Prevention through crop rotation and resistant varieties.
2. Monitoring with pheromone traps and field scouting.
3. Biological control: Trichogramma, lady beetles, neem-based pesticides.
4. Chemical control only as last resort; follow ETL (Economic Threshold Level).

### Common Pests
- **Stem borer** (Rice): Use Trichogramma egg parasitoids.
- **Fruit borer** (Tomato): Neem seed kernel extract 5%, pheromone traps.
- **Aphids**: Spray neem oil 2%, or introduce lacewings.
- **White fly**: Yellow sticky traps, neem oil spray.
